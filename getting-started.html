Ceremonial vs. Culinary

💡 Did you know there is no such thing as "ceremonial grade" matcha?
This is a term that was created by the USA to differentiate authentic and high quality matcha from non-authentic ones,
however, it is not a term commonly used by matcha connoiseurs.

Matcha Preparation Tools

This is what you'll need to make awesome matcha ☆

Tools for proper matcha preparation
Tool Name Purpose What it looks like...
Chawan The container where you prepare the matcha.
I personally prefer one with a spout for a better
pouring experience after preparation.
Bamboo Whisk (Chasen) Used to whisk the matcha powder
Strainer It helps get rid of any clumps in the matcha powder and
facilitates the creation of micro-foam during the whisking process
Measuring Spoon Used to measure out the exact quantity of desired matcha
Measuring Cup Used to measure out the exact quantity of hot water for whisking the matcha
Bamboo Ladle (optional) Used to sift the matcha powder in the strainer into the chawan
Kettle/Teapot (optional) Used to control the water temperature with more precision and pour out
